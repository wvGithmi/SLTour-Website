Wildlife Tours

Sri Lanka is one of the best countries that have nice bio-diversity in the world. You can see more wild animals including the world's largest land mammal, elephant. Sri Lanka is home to the largest seasonally recurring concentration of wild elephants found anywhere in the world. There are so many thrilling wildlife safaris you could choose from Sri Lanka. You'll be able to take lots of photographs, as well as experience our unique ever-smiling mix of people and cultures. You'll simply have a great time here! Sri Lanka has 26 Wildlife National Parks that are popular and frequently visited.

Yala National Park
Wilpattu National Park
Minneriya National Park
Udawalawe National Park
Wasgamuwa National Park

Beach Fun

Sri Lanka is a beautiful island located in the Indian ocean. Although we have numerous Golden beaches that are fringed with coconut palms making it the ideal destination for a beach holiday. One of the best parts of Sri Lankan tours is these wonderful sun-kissed sandy stretches. According to the country's coastal regions, you can choose what most suits your choice. A large percentage of luxurious resorts and villas, as well as budget hotels, are located along the coastal areas to offer tourists some of the best beach holidays in Sri Lanka. You can taste fresh seafood also.

Mirissa Beach
Beruwala Beach
Hiriketiya Beach
Passikudah Bay
Arugam Bay Beach

Heritage Sites

Sri Lanka has nearly 3000 years of rich and great history with ancient cities, palaces, temples, monasteries, hospitals, cafes, etc. UNESCO has also listed 8 World Heritage sites in Sri Lanka. These places are located in a historical location with cultural and anthropological importance. These are all very popular attractions among locals and foreigners alike. You can know the historical stories behind these ancient places and feel the beautiful Sri Lankan culture. You can plan your trip on your choice.

Sacred City of Kandy
Ancient City of Sigiriya
Golden Temple of Dambulla
Sacred City of Polonnaruwa
Sacred City of Anuradhapura
Old Town of Galle and its Fortifications

Diving Tours

The island of Sri Lanka is a paradise for diving lovers. There are many exceptional wrecks, dazzling coral reefs and, of course, an extremely rich marine life and flora. Mostly the waters of Sri Lanka are home to an incredible range of marine creatures. The great Diving spots in here make for a fun experience whether you are well-experienced in diving or are an amateur. The warm nutrient-rich waters, the hot tropical weather, and an ideal location make Sri Lanka the perfect holiday destination for lovers of diving. Around the country, there are many diving centers for beginners also.

Panadura Wreck
Hikkaduwa Scuba Diving
Kalpitiya Bar Reef Diving
Trincomalee Scuba Diving
Goda Gala Diyamba Unawatuna

Tea & Culture

Formerly known as Ceylon, Sri Lanka stands as the world’s fourth-largest producer of tea. The tea-drinking culture within this island country is strong, with Sri Lankans drinking on average three cups per day. Sri Lanka has become the producer of some of the world’s finest teas, from single origins to mixed fruit blends. Ceylon tea is one of Sri Lanka’s primary exports. It's not a complete trip without spending little time in these beautiful tea estates and feeling the climate of Hill Country of Sri Lanka. There are many tea plantations and estates that you can visit here.

Dickoya Estate
Pedro Tea Factory
Haputale Lipton's Seat
Virgin White Tea Plantation
Uva Halpewatte Tea Factory

Surfing Tours

Sri Lanka is a famous and incredible surfing destination in the world. Sri Lanka produces some good fun waves, offshore winds are super consistent during the monsoon season. The main patches for surfing are the southwest, south and east coasts. The best surf season at the southwest is from November to march and then on the east coast from May to September. The country has so much to offer for surfer lovers. There are many best surf spots in Hikkaduwa, Unawatuna, Weligama, and Arugam Bay.

Bennys in Hikkaduwa
Mirissa Bay in Weligama
Bonavista Bay in Unawatuna
Dalawella Reef in Unawatuna
Pottuvil and Whisky Point in Arugam Bay

Trekking & Hiking

You can have adventurous trekking and hiking tours in Sri Lanka's diverse and scenic hill country. There are some fantastic places where you can unleash your trekking skills. You can hike through lush-green beauty while enjoying the landscapes comprising of stunning waterfalls and green paddy fields. Sri Lanka also has over 400 waterfalls, a visit to which is included in plenty of hikes. If you are being here you are going to love exploring the alluring wilderness of Sri Lanka. There are trails for all sorts of experience, from easy walks to more intense treks.

Ella Rock
Pidurangala
Adam's Peak
Knuckles Ranges
World's End in Hortons Plain National Park

Whale Watching

The ever-smiling, mischievous dolphins will put on a show while some of the oldest and the largest sea creatures, the humpback, and the blue whales, will nonchalantly glide past you when you embark on a whale/ dolphin watching excursion off the Southern, Eastern or the West coast of Sri Lanka. Sri Lanka’s whale and dolphin populations were relatively unknown until quite recently owing to the civil war that gripped the nation’s north and east for nearly three decades. Now people can even take whale watching holidays to this paradise island and have the experience of a lifetime among some of the largest animals ever to have existed.

Whale Watching in Mirissa
Dolphin Watching in Kalpitiya
Dolphin and Whale Watching in Trincomalee

Jungle Walks

Sri Lanka is filled with beautiful and adventurous nature walks. Rainforests that are located in Sri Lanka have a mysterious, almost magical air to them and are often called the lungs of the planet for their role in absorbing carbon dioxide, greenhouse gas and producing oxygen, upon which all mankind and animals depend for survival. You can experience the real jungle camping, jungle activities, and jungle food. Also, you can have adventurous boat rides in the main rivers. You can plan your eco, nature-adventures jungle walks on your choice.

Kitulgala
Sinharaja Rain Forest
Habarana Jungle Walk
Kanneliya Forest Reserve
Beraliya Mukalana Proposed Forest Reserve

Cultural Tours

Sri Lanka is one of the few countries with a very vast and rich cultural diversity. The culture is itself very unique and thereby contributes to the Sri Lankan identity. Sri Lankan culture includes a lot of customs and rituals, which date to more than 2000 years which were handed down from generation to generation. Religion plays an important role in molding the Sri Lankan culture and traditions. The culture is often reflected by the use of art, architecture, sculptures, colorful festivals, and even food. Hospitality is also one of the prominent characteristics of the culture, making Sri Lankans one of the friendly nations in the world.

Kandy Esala Perahara
Ariyapala Mask Museum
Beeralu Lace Making in Tamarind Hill

Bird Watching

Sri Lanka is one of the best places in the world to watch birds and is truly a paradise for all Bird Watching enthusiasts. The activity becomes more common especially around the bird sanctuaries and wetland reserves in the southeast part of the island while the temperate climate is ideal for migrating birds. Sri Lanka is home to more than 400 bird species with around 23 endemics and 200 migratory birds arriving from India, Siberia, Scandinavia, and Western Europe. The best time for Bird Watching enthusiasts visits the island is towards the latter part of the year.

Habarana Forest
Bundala National Park
Kumana National Park
Minneriya National Park
Sigiriya Sanctuary Bird Watching

Ayurveda Treatments

The definition of ‘Ayurveda’ in simple terms means “the science of life”. Ayurveda represents a system of healing that has been perfected over more than five thousand years. It is famed as South Asia’s ancient health care system based on herbs and diet. Sri Lankans, in the last couple of millennia, have made use of the “user-friendly and traditional medicine – Ayurveda” which over 75% of the island’s population depend on because of its reliance on natural plants, herbs, and oils. During your holidays you can have the best ayurvedic treatments in Sri Lanka.

Ulpotha Yoga Holidays
Ayurveda Paragon Resort
Ayurveda Shunyata Village
Heritance Ayurveda Mahagedara
Siddhalepa Ayurveda Helath Resort
